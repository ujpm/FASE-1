FAST Method

Critical: Time is crucial in stroke response. Remember FAST!
Face Drooping

Ask the person to smile. Is one side drooping?

Arm Weakness

Can they raise both arms and keep them up?

Speech Difficulty

Is their speech slurred or strange?

Time to Call

If you see any of these signs, call emergency services immediately!

Time is Brain

Every minute counts in stroke treatment:

  • 2 million brain cells die every minute during a stroke
  • Treatment is most effective within 3 hours
  • Quick action can prevent permanent disability
  • Note the time symptoms first appeared

Signs & Symptoms

Vision Problems
  • Blurred vision
  • Double vision
  • Loss of vision
  • One-sided vision loss
Balance Issues
  • Sudden dizziness
  • Loss of coordination
  • Trouble walking
  • Unexplained falls
Other Symptoms
  • Severe headache
  • Confusion
  • Numbness
  • Difficulty understanding

Emergency Response

Immediate Actions

  1. Call Emergency Services

    Dial emergency number immediately upon recognizing symptoms.

  2. Note the Time

    Record when symptoms first appeared.

  3. Keep Calm

    Reassure the person and keep them still.

  4. Monitor

    Check breathing and consciousness.

Recovery Position

Step 1

Position arm at right angle

Step 2

Place other hand under cheek

Step 3

Bend top leg at knee

Step 4

Roll onto side

Prevention & Risk

Modifiable Risks
  • High blood pressure
  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • High cholesterol
Non-modifiable Risks
  • Age
  • Family history
  • Gender
  • Previous stroke
Prevention Strategies
  • Regular blood pressure checks
  • Healthy diet
  • Regular exercise
  • Quit smoking
  • Limit alcohol

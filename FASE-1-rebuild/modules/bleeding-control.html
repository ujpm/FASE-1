Types of Bleeding

Critical: Identifying the type of bleeding is crucial for proper treatment.
Arterial Bleeding
  • Bright red blood
  • Spurting with heartbeat
  • Most dangerous type
  • Requires immediate action
Venous Bleeding
  • Dark red blood
  • Steady flow
  • Less pressure than arterial
  • Still requires quick response
Capillary Bleeding
  • Oozing blood
  • Slow flow
  • Common in scrapes
  • Usually minor

Pressure Application Techniques

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Expose the Wound

    Remove or cut away clothing to clearly see the injury.

  2. Apply Direct Pressure

    Use sterile gauze or clean cloth, apply firm, direct pressure to the wound.

  3. Maintain Pressure

    Continue pressure for at least 10-15 minutes without lifting to check.

  4. Add Layers

    If blood soaks through, add more layers without removing original dressing.

Tourniquet Usage

Warning: Only use a tourniquet when direct pressure fails or is not possible.

Proper Application

  1. Position

    Place 2-3 inches above wound, not on a joint.

  2. Tighten

    Pull strap tight and secure with velcro.

  3. Twist Windlass

    Turn until bleeding stops.

  4. Secure and Time

    Lock windlass and note application time.

Time Critical: Note the exact time of application. A tourniquet should not be left on for more than 2 hours.

Types of Seizures

Important: Different types of seizures require different responses.
Generalized Tonic-Clonic
  • Loss of consciousness
  • Muscle stiffening
  • Jerking movements
  • Most recognizable type
Absence Seizures
  • Brief loss of awareness
  • Appear to be staring
  • No convulsions
  • Common in children
Focal Seizures
  • Affects one part of brain
  • May spread to both sides
  • Various symptoms
  • Person may be aware

Warning Signs

Aura

Visual changes or strange sensations

Confusion

Sudden disorientation

Muscle Changes

Sudden stiffness or twitching

Speech Issues

Difficulty speaking

Do's During a Seizure
  • Keep calm
  • Clear the area
  • Protect the head
  • Time the seizure
Don'ts During a Seizure
  • Don't restrain them
  • Don't put anything in mouth
  • Don't try to stop movements
  • Don't give food or water

Emergency Response

Remember: Most seizures end naturally within a few minutes.

Immediate Response Steps

  1. Ensure Safety

    Move hazards away and protect from injury.

  2. Note the Time

    Begin timing the seizure when symptoms start.

  3. Protect Head

    Place something soft under their head.

  4. Roll to Recovery

    Once movements stop, roll them onto their side.

Types of Injuries

Important: Proper identification of injury type is crucial for appropriate treatment.
Closed Fractures
  • Bone broken but skin intact
  • Swelling and bruising
  • Limited movement
  • Pain on movement
Open Fractures
  • Bone pierces skin
  • Risk of infection high
  • Requires immediate care
  • Control bleeding first
Sprains
  • Ligament damage
  • Immediate swelling
  • Range of grades (1-3)
  • Common in joints
Look For
  • Deformity
  • Swelling
  • Bruising
  • Open wounds
Feel For
  • Tenderness
  • Crepitus
  • Abnormal movement
  • Temperature changes

Assessment & RICE

Rest

Stop using injured area

Ice

20 minutes every 2 hours

Compression

Wrap with elastic bandage

Elevation

Raise above heart level

Warning: Seek immediate medical attention for:
  • Open fractures
  • Severe deformity
  • Loss of circulation
  • Numbness or tingling

Splinting Techniques

Basic Splinting Steps

  1. Check Circulation

    Assess pulse and sensation before and after splinting.

  2. Prepare Materials

    Gather rigid support and padding materials.

  3. Support Joints

    Include joints above and below injury site.

  4. Secure Splint

    Use ties or bandages to hold splint in place.

Do's
  • Pad bony prominences
  • Check circulation regularly
  • Keep splint snug but not tight
  • Immobilize joints
Don'ts
  • Don't attempt to realign bones
  • Don't remove shoes/clothing
  • Don't delay medical care
  • Don't apply direct pressure

Transport & Aftercare

Safe Transport Guidelines
  • Keep injured area immobilized during transport
  • Use proper lifting techniques
  • Support the injury during movement
  • Consider using a stretcher for leg injuries

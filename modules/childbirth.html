Labor Signs

Important: Call emergency services immediately when labor begins unexpectedly.
Timing Contractions
  • Regular intervals
  • Increasing frequency
  • Growing intensity
  • Duration of each
Water Breaking
  • Clear fluid release
  • Continuous leaking
  • Color important
  • Time of rupture
Warning Signs
  • Heavy bleeding
  • Severe pain
  • High fever
  • Decreased movement

Stages of Labor

First Stage
Duration: 12-24 hours (first pregnancy)

Cervix dilates from 0 to 10 centimeters

Second Stage
Duration: 30 minutes to 3 hours

Active pushing and delivery of baby

Third Stage
Duration: 5-30 minutes

Delivery of placenta

Delivery Process

Essential Supplies
  • Clean towels/blankets
  • Sterile gloves
  • Clean scissors
  • String/cord clamps
  • Plastic sheet
Hygiene Items
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Soap
  • Clean water
  • Antiseptic solution

Emergency Delivery Steps

  1. Prepare the Area

    Create a clean, warm environment with good lighting.

  2. Position the Mother

    Help her lie on her back with knees bent and feet flat.

  3. Support the Head

    As baby crowns, support the head but don't pull.

  4. Check for Cord

    Ensure umbilical cord is not wrapped around neck.

Emergency Signs

Call emergency services immediately if you observe:

  • Excessive bleeding
  • Breech presentation
  • Prolapsed cord
  • Mother loses consciousness

Newborn Care

Immediate Care
  • Clear airways
  • Dry and warm baby
  • Assess breathing
  • Skin-to-skin contact
Cord Care
  • Wait for pulsation to stop
  • Clamp/tie cord twice
  • Cut between clamps
  • Check for bleeding

Maternal Care

Post-Delivery Care

  1. Monitor Bleeding

    Watch for excessive blood loss and clots.

  2. Deliver Placenta

    Wait for natural delivery, don't pull.

  3. Massage Uterus

    Gentle massage to help contraction.

  4. Keep Warm

    Maintain mother's body temperature.

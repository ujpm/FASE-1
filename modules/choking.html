Identifying Choking Signs

Important: Quick recognition of choking signs is crucial for immediate response.
Universal Sign

Hands clutched to throat

Cannot Speak

Inability to talk or make sounds

Breathing Difficulty

Weak or no breathing sounds

Face Color

Turning blue or pale

Heimlich Maneuver Technique

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Position

    Stand behind the person and wrap your arms around their waist.

  2. Hand Position

    Make a fist with one hand, place it thumb-side against their upper abdomen, just above the navel.

  3. Grasp and Pull

    Grasp your fist with your other hand and give quick, upward thrusts.

  4. Repeat

    Continue thrusts until the object is expelled or the person becomes unconscious.

Helping Children and Infants

Remember: Techniques vary based on age and size of the victim.
Children (1-8 years)
  • Use less force than for adults
  • Kneel behind if necessary
  • Use two fingers for back blows
  • Alternate with abdominal thrusts
Infants (Under 1 year)
  • Support head and neck
  • Give 5 back blows
  • Turn over and give 5 chest thrusts
  • Use very gentle force
